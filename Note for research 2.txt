Research Question
How do word frequency and word length influence reaction times in a Vietnamese lexical decision task, and do these factors interact?

Dependent Variable (DV)
Reaction time (in milliseconds) measured from stimulus onset to participant response
(Accuracy recorded as secondary measure)

1. Processing level (DATA)
split theo space
→ “học sinh” = 2 syllables
2. Measurement (VARIABLE)
word length = number of syllables
3. Lexical level (MEANING)
“học sinh” vẫn là 1 word về mặt nghĩa

Bạn đang test:

👉 Người Việt đọc chữ như thế nào?

Cụ thể:

từ ngắn (1 syllable) → nhanh hơn?
từ dài (3 syllables) → chậm hơn?
từ phổ biến → nhanh hơn?
từ hiếm → chậm hơn?

Independent Variables (IVs)

Word frequency (log-transformed, continuous)
Word length (to be defined: letters OR syllables — not yet fixed)
Interaction: frequency × length

Methodology Overview

Experimental design using a lexical decision task
Participants: 40–60 native Vietnamese speakers
Stimuli: ~200 real words + ~200 pseudowords
Tool: PsychoPy
Procedure: fixation cross → stimulus → key press (word / non-word)
Analysis: linear mixed-effects model (lme4 in R)
Fixed effects: frequency, length, interaction
Random effects: participants, items
Data preprocessing: remove incorrect responses, remove outliers, log-transform RT

Language: Vietnamese (Chữ Quốc Ngữ)
Task: visual lexical decision task
DV: reaction time in milliseconds
Secondary DV: accuracy
IV1: log word frequency
IV2: word length = number of syllables
Interaction: frequency × syllable length
Participants: 40–60 native Vietnamese speakers
Stimuli: real words + pseudowords
Tool: PsychoPy
Analysis: linear mixed-effects model in R
