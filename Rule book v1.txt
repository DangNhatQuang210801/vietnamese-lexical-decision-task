Rulebook v1 — Selecting Vietnamese Lexical Stimuli

Objective
The goal is to select lexical items from a Vietnamese corpus for use as stimuli in a lexical decision task. The selected items must be meaningful, stable in form, and appropriate for a syllable-based writing system.
Unit of analysis
In this project, a “word” is operationalized as a high-frequency syllable n-gram that approximates a lexical unit.
Only bigrams (two-syllable units) are used as primary stimuli. Trigrams are excluded due to segmentation noise.
Data source
The corpus is collected from online Vietnamese news articles.
All texts are cleaned to remove HTML, metadata, and non-linguistic noise.
Tokenization is performed at the syllable level, followed by n-gram generation.
Selection criteria

4.1 No stopwords
An item must not contain function words such as conjunctions, particles, or grammatical markers.
Examples to exclude: và, là, của, có, trong, khi, nếu.

4.2 No boundary function words
An item must not begin or end with a function word.
Examples to exclude: sau khi, tuy nhiên.

4.3 Clear lexical meaning
An item must have a stable and independent lexical meaning.
Examples to include: công ty, gia đình, thị trường.
Examples to exclude: có thể, thực hiện (context-dependent or function-like expressions).

4.4 Preference for noun or noun-like units
To ensure consistency across stimuli, noun phrases or noun-like expressions are preferred.
Examples to include: học sinh, cơ quan, thế giới.
Verb phrases are excluded unless a broader design is explicitly justified.

4.5 No fragmented sequences
Items that are fragments of longer expressions must be excluded.
Examples to exclude: đốc công ty, báo người lao.

4.6 Frequency threshold
Items must fall within a high-frequency range to ensure recognizability.
Only items within a predefined top frequency band (e.g., top 500–2000) are selected.

Exclusion categories
The following categories are systematically removed:
Function words
Discourse markers (e.g., tuy nhiên, mặt khác)
Verb phrases (if restricting to noun-based stimuli)
Fragmented n-grams
Optional: named entities, depending on experimental design
Justification
Vietnamese orthography separates syllables rather than words, which complicates word identification based on whitespace. High-frequency bigrams provide a practical approximation of lexical units. A rule-based filtering procedure ensures that selected items reflect meaningful and stable lexical representations suitable for psycholinguistic experiments.
Final output
The final stimulus set consists of approximately 120 to 160 bigrams.
Each item includes the following attributes:
lexical_item
frequency
log_frequency
syllable_length (fixed at 2)
